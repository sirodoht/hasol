Nutcroft Handbook

Knives

Our knives are the most prized possession of our esteemed kitchen. We hold them very highly in our modus operandi and there are specific practices that one should follow when interacting with them. Namely:

  1. Do not use the harsh green side of the sponge because it destroys the amazing anti-stick coating of the knives.
  2. Do not put the knives into the washing machines because lots of water makes them less sharp.
  3. Do not put knives with other knives or with other cutlery because it’s like a sword fight and they get damaged.
  4. Do not place knives inside the washing cutlery rack because it’s made out of metal and it will be like a sword fight again.

Finally, we are racist towards our knives as all these practices are about coloured knives. We don’t care about our generic gold and silver knives.

The guardian of the operations around knives is Flo.

Pans

We have a wide range of pans, which, in order to take care of, and because they are anti-stick pans, we should not use the harsh green side of the sponge on the inside side of the pans.

In addition, we do not put them inside the dish washing machine (or any washing machine actually).

Cleaning

We clean our revered house every week and we are very meticulous in keeping it clean every time we interact with any room or piece of furniture. We keep our cleaning rota at nutcroft.com and one can also subscribe for email updates every Monday 8am exactly.

The common-for-all weekly house cleaning tasks are:

  1. Kitchen part 1
  2. Kitchen part 2
  3. Living room
  4. Corridors
  5. Bins
  6. Vacuum cleaner
  7. Front porch

In addition to these we also have the toilets which are cleaned by the people that share them.

Bikes

How could we not have a special relationship with the past, present, and future of urban transportation? The best, coolest, most efficient, most environmentally friendly, most human friendly, sum positive means of transportation is highly regarded in the Nutcroft Foundation. We have resident expert bicycle knowledge that we share amongst us, as well as, all the necessary tools to fix and maintain one’s bicycle.

Guests policy

We welcome all Nutcroft residents to bring their guests, though due to corona we have established that one should inform the Foundation beforehand.

The process for long term guests (more than 1-2 weeks) is open to discussion.

Washing machines

We have a high quality fully automated dish washing machine that we use daily. Some things can’t go inside such as the coloured knives and the pans.

We also have two clothes washing machines. The one on the ground floor is currently facing some issues and may not start if angry. The one in the kitchen is super friendly though, starts immediately on the touch of a button.

Hoover

We have a state of the art vacuum cleaner which we call Hoover, or just H. The official residing place of H is in the living room while H can sometimes be found hanging around outside the kitchen.

H has many functions and we keep learning new ones as time passes and we interact more.

H has recently had an illness in which filters were lost and fans got infiltrated by dust. Thankfully we fitted H with new filters as soon as possible.

The guardian of the Hoover is Karen.

Garden

We grow fruit and vegetable in the garden. There is also a newly built fire pit which we plan to use in the winter in case the heating breaks down.

Bins

We love bins (we have 8 in total) but we are also racist towards them. We have 4 of them in the kitchen. Namely:

  • Blue: recycling
  • Black: general waste
  • Small brown (small compost): vegan food + crushed egg shells, but not deep fried stuff or garlic
  • Big brown: non-vegan food, deep fried food, garlic, non-crushed eggshells

For the compost, you can find more information on what is compostable in the flyer on the tall fridge.

The Southwark Council collects some of our garbage every Thursday. Depending on the week it may collect either brown + blue or brown + black. One can find the annual schedule on the tall fridge.

Every Wednesday night, the resident in charge of bins makes sure that the appropriate garbage has been put out and the appropriately coloured bins are outside of our front porch.

Finally, we have the big compost in the garden, which we put the small compost’s contents.

Alcohol

How could we be true revolutionaries if we didn’t consume one of the most ancient and dangerous substances? Alcohol, and specifically beer and mezcal are the two official drinks of the Nutcroft Foundation. While we love those timelessly, we continuously try new ones to broaden our horizons.

Parties

We hold multi-annual Nutcroft conferences which denote milestones in our society’s journey throughout time. Due to corona we had to cut back on those but we do have the next one scheduled for July 11th.

Meetings

The holy process of Nutcroft is our house meetings. We used to hold them every one or two weeks, but due to corona times, we now hold ad-hoc meetings.

The meetings take place in the kitchen, sitting around the holy table. Every meeting has a moderator and a meeting notes author.

The moderator's job is to control who talks so that there is equality amongst everybody in talking and hearing time. In order to do this efficiently, and without talking, we have adopted some signs to convey our thoughts in a muted fasion. Specifically:

  • To convey aggreement, one raises their hands with their palms in a spherical shape and shakes them circularly, like Helena is doing here.
  • To convey disagreement and respond to a point, one raises their hands with their palms in a straight shape, while also holding them parallel to one another. Brad shows how here, but remember to use both hands (in constrast to Brad).
  • To convey disagreement and respond to a direct point, one uses their thumb and index and shapes them like a lobster's claw. Here's an example of how it should like.

The moderator closely watches everybody and when somebody makes a response, they make a mental note and give permission to talk to the person who spoke the least, or however else they choose to balance the discussion.

The meetings notes author's job is to capture what has been said in the meeting so that we can refer to it later. It is a job of utmost importance as the meeting notes will be the truth that will help us solidify our decisions. Ideally, everybody should read the meeting notes after the meeting and sign off on them.

Mates disagreements

It is unavoidable to have disagreements between the mates of the house at some point. Thus it is important to have a high quality process of solving them.

Our process is as follows. The person who has the disagreement talks with the person that they disagree with. In case this fails, then the person who has the disagreement approaches a third person and all three together try to solve the issue at hand. If this process also fails, then the person who has the disagreement raises the issue on a house meeting (or asks for one if there aren't any scheduled).

It is important to externalize our disagreements as the person on the other side might not be aware of the issue they might cause, while also it might be easier for them to rectify than assumed.

Consensus

We try to reach consensus in all the decisions we make. The basis of that is a common goal we all share: high quality co-living.
